Muhammad Ali

The Greatest Boxer of All Time

Introduction

Muhammad Ali (1942–2016), born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr., was an iconic American boxer, philanthropist, and social activist. Known as "The Greatest," he captured the world’s attention with his unmatched skill, charismatic personality, and unwavering commitment to justice. A three-time heavyweight champion, Ali’s fights, like the “Rumble in the Jungle” and the “Thrilla in Manila,” are legendary. Beyond the ring, he became a global symbol of courage, standing against racial inequality, war, and oppression. His legacy endures as a champion both inside and outside the boxing ring.

Key Moments

  • 1942 Born in Louisville, Kentucky.
  • 1960 Won the Olympic gold medal in boxing.
  • 1964 Became World Heavyweight Champion after defeating Sonny Liston.
  • 1967 Refused military service, famously declaring, "I ain't got no quarrel with the Vietcong."
  • 1971 Lost and regained the World Heavyweight Title after defeating Joe Frazier in the "Fight of the Century."
  • 1974 Won the "Rumble in the Jungle" against George Foreman.
  • 1981 Announced his retirement from boxing for the third and final time.
  • 2016 Passed away, leaving a lasting legacy in both boxing and social activism.
